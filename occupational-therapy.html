Pediatric Occupational Therapy

Empowering Children to Achieve Independence in Daily Activities

What is Occupational Therapy?

Pediatric Occupational Therapy (OT) helps children develop the skills needed for daily living and independence. Our therapy focuses on improving physical, cognitive, and sensory abilities to help children participate fully in school, play, and social activities.

Key Focus Areas

  • Fine & Gross Motor Skills
  • Sensory Processing
  • Self-Care Skills
  • Social Interaction
  • Cognitive Development

Why It's Important

  • Improves school performance
  • Enhances play skills
  • Boosts self-confidence
  • Promotes independence
  • Supports sensory integration

Common Conditions We Address

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
  • Developmental Delays
  • Sensory Processing Disorders
  • Cerebral Palsy
  • Down Syndrome
  • Attention Deficit Disorders

Our Therapeutic Approaches

Sensory Integration Therapy

Helping children process and respond to sensory information appropriately

Play-Based Therapy

Using therapeutic play to develop essential skills

Fine Motor Skill Development

Improving hand-eye coordination and dexterity

Quick Facts

Typical Session Includes:
  • 45-60 minute sessions
  • Individualized activities
  • Parent/caregiver education
  • Progress tracking
When to Consider OT?
  • Difficulty with handwriting
  • Sensory sensitivities
  • Poor social skills
  • Coordination challenges
